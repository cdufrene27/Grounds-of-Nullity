Canonical Trial Progress Tracker

This interactive roadmap guides advocates through all five phases of canonical marriage nullity trials. Click on each phase below to expand detailed checklists, timelines, and strategic considerations to ensure you're maximizing every opportunity to serve your petitioner's interests.

⚠️ Key Principle: Each phase has specific advocate opportunities and deadlines. Missing procedural windows can severely impact your petitioner's case. Use this tracker to stay ahead of critical deadlines and strategic decision points.

Preliminary Investigation ▼

Collect information and prepare libellus with grounds of nullity

Case Assignment Checklist

  • Confirm case receipt via TEAMS upload
  • Notify tribunal staff if received directly for tracking

Libellus Preparation Steps

  • Review case counselor's interview materials
  • Verify actual interview occurred (not just questionnaire)
  • Convert old forms to current libellus format
  • Draft initial libellus based on interview

Advocate-Petitioner Meeting

Meeting Options:

  • Schedule in-person meeting
  • Set up video call
  • Arrange phone conference

Meeting Objectives:

  • Confirm petitioner understands proposed ground
  • Help identify additional supporting facts
  • Finalize witness list with petitioner
  • Update libellus based on meeting insights

Difficult Respondent Planning

Warning: Address respondent issues early to avoid delays

Preventive Measures:

  • Develop alternative address proof strategies
  • Consider forum shopping options
  • Prepare Signatura competence transfer if needed
  • Use diplomatic libellus wording
  • Coordinate with civil protection orders

Available Restrictions:

  • Contact info redaction (just cause required)
  • Libellus withholding until testimony (grave cause)
  • Act withholding from respondent (most grave danger)
  • Guardian appointment for incapacitated

Unlocatable Respondent Process

Petitioner writes detailed last contact letter
Submit $100 tribunal search fee
Tribunal conducts comprehensive search
30-day waiting period after notices
Declaration of unlocatable status
Note: This process can delay case 6+ months

Final Submission Checklist

  • Password protect or secure transmission
  • Obtain petitioner signature
  • Attempt respondent co-signature if possible
  • Coordinate submission with case counselor
  • Verify all required documents included

Red Flags Requiring Action

  • Inadequate interviews: Demand re-interview
  • Missing documentation: Verify all collected
  • Weak grounds: Ensure sufficient factual support
  • Jurisdictional issues: Confirm competence early

Introduction of the Cause ▼

Establish competence, notify parties, formulate doubt

Libellus Admission Review

Tribunal Verifies:

  • Competence per c. 1672 (domicile, marriage location, proof location)
  • Standing per c. 1674 (living spouses, Catholic party)
  • Formal completeness of libellus
  • Basis assessment for potential success

Advocate Actions at Admission

Tip: You have 10 days to appeal libellus rejection
  • Review decree of admission carefully
  • Object to personnel appointments if needed
  • Respond to alternative ground suggestions
  • State position on briefer vs. ordinary process

Dilatory Exceptions (Must raise before joinder)

Available Challenges:

  • Competence challenges - tribunal lacks jurisdiction
  • Personnel disqualification - conflicts of interest
  • Standing issues - party lacks right to petition

Disqualification Grounds (c. 1448):

  • Consanguinity/affinity relationships
  • Trusteeship/guardianship relationships
  • Close personal relationships
  • Great animosity toward parties
  • Financial interest in outcome
  • Incompatible prior role

Respondent Citation Process

Certified mail to provided address
Respondent receives response form
Respondent states position on nullity
May propose alternative grounds
May request advocate appointment

Formula of the Doubt - Critical Review

Critical: The formula limits tribunal investigation scope

Verification Checklist:

  • Verify correct canonical terminology used
  • Confirm proper party attribution
  • Ensure all intended grounds included
  • Object within 10 days if incorrect

Sample Formula: "Whether the nullity of marriage is proven in the cause on account of a grave defect of discretion of judgment on the part of the petitioner."

Post-Joinder Actions

  • Final judge challenge opportunity
  • Verify ordinary vs. briefer designation
  • Begin identifying additional evidence needs
  • Start preliminary witness contact
  • Organize supporting documentation
2

Instruction of the Cause ▼

Gather evidence through depositions, experts, and documents

Decree of Instruction Response

  • Review auditor assignment - object if needed
  • Check authorized witness list
  • Evaluate expert appointment (psychological cases)
  • Submit additional witness proposals
  • Provide specific questions (articuli argumentorum)
  • Signal intent to attend depositions

Witness Management Strategy

Witness Eligibility Review:

Excluded: Minors under 14, limited capacity, judges, advocates, confession-related

Witness Preparation:

  • Contact witnesses about scheduling
  • Explain deposition process
  • Remind about truth obligations
  • Discuss key facts they can address

Strategic Considerations:

  • Propose character witnesses if needed
  • Consider ex officio witness requests
  • Plan for hostile witness handling

Deposition Attendance

Best Practice: Attend all key depositions via Teams or in-person

During Depositions:

  • Note witness demeanor and credibility
  • Prepare follow-up questions for judge
  • Request clarifications on unclear responses
  • Monitor for inconsistencies

Document Submission Process

Authentication Requirements:

  • Submit originals or authentic copies
  • Obtain notary authentication if needed
  • Prepare witness verification for private docs
  • Include signed affidavits for private documents
Remember: Public documents self-authenticate; private documents need support

Expert Process Management

Advocate Involvement:

  • Challenge appointment if "manifestly useless"
  • Object to specific expert selection
  • Submit questions for expert consideration
  • Consider private expert at party expense
  • Request expert-party interview if beneficial
  • Attend clarification sessions if held

Publication of Proofs - Critical Phase

Critical: First complete view of opponent's case

Evidence Assessment:

  • Compare libellus claims to actual evidence
  • Evaluate witness credibility and consistency
  • Analyze expert conclusions
  • Identify contrary evidence

Additional Proof Options:

  • Request supplemental witness questions
  • Propose new witnesses
  • Seek additional expert clarification
  • Submit newly discovered documents
  • Consider ground change if evidence supports

Warning Signs Requiring Action

  • Expert contradiction: No anomaly found
  • Simulation denial: Petitioner denies in testimony
  • Major contradictions: Evidence conflicts with claims
  • Lack of corroboration: Petitioner testimony alone

Pre-Conclusion Checklist

  • Verify all beneficial evidence submitted
  • Address evidentiary gaps
  • Submit formal requests for post-conclusion evidence if needed
  • Begin organizing evidence for brief preparation

Discussion of the Cause ▼

Submit written briefs and participate in oral arguments

Brief Preparation Timeline

Receive decree with deadline
Submit initial brief
Exchange briefs with opposition
Submit reply brief
Possible second reply round
Defender gets final reply

Brief Structure Requirements

Essential Components:

  • Basic Facts (optional but helpful)
  • Reasons in Law (if legal issues present)
  • Reasons in Fact (heart of the brief)
  • Professional conclusion

Writing Standards:

  • Use complete sentences
  • Maintain professional tone
  • Write from advocate perspective
  • Sign with advocate name

Psychological Cases - Brief Organization

Follow Proof Schema:

  1. Causes of Anomaly
    • Background factors
    • Family dysfunction
    • Trauma or crisis
    • Supporting testimony
  2. Effects of Anomaly
    • Employment issues
    • Relationship patterns
    • Decision-making problems
    • Multiple witness corroboration
  3. Expert Analysis
    • Professional diagnosis
    • Supporting evidence
    • Address any limitations

Simulation Cases - Brief Organization

Follow Proof Schema:

  1. Motive to Simulate
    • Remote motives (background)
    • Proximate motives (specific reasons)
  2. Statements Indicating Simulation
    • Judicial confessions
    • Extrajudicial statements
    • Tempore non suspecto statements
  3. Behavioral Evidence
    • Pre-wedding actions
    • Post-wedding behavior
    • Pattern of conduct

Evidence Integration Checklist

  • Quote relevant witness testimony with citations
  • Reference specific documents
  • Cite expert conclusions and reasoning
  • Note credibility factors
  • Address contradictory evidence
  • Anticipate Defender arguments

Reply Brief Strategy

Remember: Defender gets last word - make your replies count
  • Focus on specific opposition arguments
  • Don't repeat entire case
  • Highlight supporting evidence
  • Distinguish witness reliability
  • Refute alternative explanations
  • Address likely Defender responses preemptively

Oral Discussion Preparation

If Ordered by Judge:

  • Review specific discussion points
  • Prepare concise arguments
  • Anticipate judge questions
  • Plan closing statement
  • Remember Defender speaks last

Standards of Proof Reminders

Moral Certainty Required: Higher than preponderance or clear and convincing

Strengthening Weak Cases:

  • Emphasize internal consistency
  • Highlight character witnesses
  • Show tempore non suspecto support
  • Use circumstantial evidence
  • Address bias concerns directly

Decision & Recourse ▼

Judicial deliberation, sentence, and possible appeals

Decision Process Timeline

Judges meet (typically twice monthly)
Individual judge preparation
Judicial conference and deliberation
Sentence drafting and approval
Publication to advocates

Sentence Review Checklist

  • Review case identification and history
  • Analyze the law section
  • Study evidence analysis carefully
  • Understand judicial reasoning
  • Check for vetitum (remarriage restrictions)
  • Note any conditions imposed

Negative Decision Response

Important: Tribunal gives advance notice - notify client personally

Client Communication:

  • Contact client before official notice
  • Explain decision reasoning
  • Discuss appeal options
  • Set realistic expectations
  • Provide emotional support

Appeal Options

Available Recourses:

  • Standard appeal to second instance
  • Complaint of nullity (procedural violations)
  • Restitutio in integrum (extraordinary remedy)
  • Nova propositio (new grounds)

Appeal Deadlines:

Critical: 15 days to file appeal notice

Affirmative Decision Actions

  • Explain decision to client
  • Address any vetitum requirements
  • Discuss condition fulfillment if applicable
  • Await automatic review confirmation
  • Prepare for possible Defender appeal

Educational Value Review

Professional Development: Use sentence as learning tool
  • Compare brief arguments to judicial reasoning
  • Note which evidence was most persuasive
  • Identify areas for improvement
  • Apply lessons to future cases

Post-Decision Client Support

  • Schedule follow-up meeting
  • Discuss next steps (marriage prep, counseling)
  • Provide closure and pastoral support
  • Maintain confidentiality obligations
  • Document case completion
